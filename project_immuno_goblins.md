## Cast
- **Alvin = IgA** — Pool lifeguard, moonlights as wet bar bouncer. Most abundant Ab by mass, dominates mucosal surfaces.
- **Gryf = IgG** — Homecoming king, really popular. Most abundant in serum, crosses placenta, goes rogue in lupus.
- **Merlin = IgM** — Class president, gets nose into everything. First responder, pentameric, generalist tagger.
- **Eric = IgE** — Daytime florist, broke from buying Zyrtec/Flonase. Allergy/parasite Ab. Florist = occupational IgE sensitization. Making own problem worse.
- **Darryl = IgD** — Sometimes shows up, brings snacks. Nobody fully understands IgD, debated for 60 years.
- **Complements** — Janitors. Tag, recruit, punch holes via MAC.
- **Macrophages** — Garbage men. "Big eater" in Greek.
- **Betty** — A B cell. Antibody factory. Bad Bettys slipped through tolerance. Benlysta tells bad Bettys party's over.
- **Professor lymphocytes** — Memory cells. Long-term grudge-holders. Vaccines generate professors without the disease.
- **Freshman lymphocytes** — Naive cells. Most never get tenure.
- **ENA/ANA cascade** — "The seventeen hundred dwarves of autoimmunity." Tolkien-scale.

## Open Casting Slots
T regulatory cells (bouncer/de-escalator), Neutrophils (kamikaze, leave NETs → lupus), Dendritic cells (gossips), Mast cells (firework-launchers, Eric's friends), NK cells, CD4+ helpers, CD8+ cytotoxic, Interleukins (characters vs weather TBD), Cytokines broadly.

**Why:** Relational scaffold makes abstract immunology memorable. Works especially well for autistic learners and kids. Closest existing thing is Cells at Work! anime but that's entertainment-first and doesn't cover autoimmunity, diagnostic limitations, or patient experience.
